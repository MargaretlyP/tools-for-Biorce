TL;DR — Question 1
What is the size of the opportunity in Japan and how does it compare to the US/EU market?
Japan's pharma market ($88B) is the world's 3rd largest, its AI clinical trials segment $20-50M est. (2026) is a fraction of the US (~$2B) and EU (~$750M). What makes it interesting is what sits underneath: 12–18 month PMDA timelines, $38M median Phase 3 costs, a universal 80%+ trial recruitment failure amplified by Japan's aging demographics, and zero AI protocol tools addressing any of it. The market isn't large. The pain is.

TL;DR — Question 2
What do we need to know about the Japanese life sciences industry?
Japan has mature buyers ($88B pharma, 70+ JPMA firms), a government pushing AI (Society 5.0, $2M+ AMED grants), and a regulatory process that creates the exact pain AI prep tools solve. But: consensus culture doubles sales cycles, bilingual PMDA talent takes 6 months to hire, and only 18.6% of companies have adopted digital trial tools. The opportunity is structural. So are the barriers.

TL;DR — Question 3
How can we successfully enter the market and what do we need to keep in mind?
60–70% of foreign biotech entries to Japan fail — almost entirely from prep failures, not product failures. The observed pattern for successful entrants: JETRO soft landing → CRO partnership → grant-funded pilot → enterprise scale, with a 6–12 month path to first revenue. Biggest documented killers: skipping PMDA pre-consultation, English-only materials, and ignoring consensus culture. Bilingual PMDA talent takes 6+ months to hire. The playbook exists. 60–70% still fail. The difference is prep.

READING THE STRATEGY

Japan market entry has a documented 60–70% failure rate for foreign entrants. The failures are almost entirely prep failures — wrong documents, no local entity, skipping consensus culture, hiring too late.

The successful pattern is well-documented: JETRO for soft landing, CRO partnership for regulatory access, AMED grants for de-risked pilots, 8–12 stakeholder nemawashi before any deal. A 14-month European biopharma benchmark anchors the timeline. Japan's 2x Phase 3 cost differential establishes why efficiency tools command premium pricing.

The scarcest resource is bilingual PMDA talent (6–9 months to hire). The biggest cultural gap is nemawashi. The most common fatal error is skipping PMDA pre-consultation. These are facts from documented cases — what to do with them is a decision this briefing doesn't make.
